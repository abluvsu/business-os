# BRAND FOUNDATION

This is the single source of truth for the Business OS brand. Every marketing asset, landing
page, social post, blog article, email, UI copy string, presentation, and future AI agent
must follow this document.

This is not marketing content. It is the operating manual for how the company presents
itself for the next several years.

**Research basis:** The brand principles below are informed by studying how Stripe, Linear,
Notion, Figma, Vercel, and GitHub present themselves - their shared commitment to restraint,
clarity, craft, and calm confidence. We extracted the _principles_ and wrote our own language.
No phrasing from those companies appears here.

This document does not redefine company philosophy, terminology, priorities, or history. Those live
in the Company Foundation layer (`COMPANY_CONTEXT.md`, `CURRENT_SPRINT.md`,
`CURRENT_PRIORITIES.md`, `GLOSSARY.md`, `DECISIONS.md`). Where this document touches them, it
references them.

---

## 1. Brand Purpose

Make a solo founder feel understood and in control of their marketing - without hype, without
complexity, and without giving up their data.

The brand exists to reduce noise in a founder's life, not to add to it.

---

## 2. Brand Promise

The promise we keep, internally and externally:

> Business OS is calm, private, and honest. It explains your business in plain language, and it
> never acts on your business without you.

This is a commitment we measure ourselves against, not a line for an advertisement.

---

## 3. Brand Positioning

We sit against three things:

- **Dashboards** - they overwhelm the non-technical founder with metrics they must interpret
  alone. We answer in conversation.
- **Agencies and consultants** - too expensive and too slow for a solo founder. We are the
  calm second opinion that lives on their machine.
- **"AI-powered" noise** - vague, hype-driven claims that say nothing. We name the concrete
  help.

We are explicitly **not** a CRM, an automation platform, or a multi-agent platform. Our
position is narrow and confident: the workspace that already understands your business.

For: the solo founder running their own marketing, with low technical ability and no budget for
a team.

---

## 4. Brand Personality

Verbatim from `COMPANY_CONTEXT.md`, because the brand and the product share one voice:

- Calm
- Clear
- Practical
- Trustworthy
- Intelligent
- Minimal

The brand speaks like a knowledgeable friend who respects the founder's time. It is never
salesy, never loud, never buzzword-heavy.

---

## 5. Brand Values

These are the company principles expressed as how we present ourselves. Definitions of the
principles live in `COMPANY_CONTEXT.md §8` and are not restated here.

- **Privacy by default** (Local First) - we present local, on-device data as a strength, never
  an apology.
- **Honesty over hype** (Compliance by Design, Cost First) - we say what the product does and
  what it costs.
- **Simplicity as respect** (Everything should feel simple) - if a founder must learn something
  to benefit, the brand failed.
- **Founder autonomy** (Founder Control) - the founder owns their data and decisions; the brand
  never implies we run their business for them.
- **Context over noise** (Context Before AI) - we lead with understanding, not with model talk.
- **Portability** (Zero Vendor Lock-in) - we make leaving easy, and say so.

---

## 6. Emotional Response We Want To Create

When a founder meets Business OS, we want them to feel:

- **Understood** - "this thing gets my business."
- **Calm** - "finally, something quiet."
- **In control** - "my data stays mine."
- **Confident** - "I can answer this marketing question."

The single feeling to engineer for is relief. The founder's day is noisy; we are the quiet room.

---

## 7. Audience Perception

How we want the Founder to describe us, in their own words:

- "A private tool that respects me and my data."
- "Calmer than a dashboard, clearer than a spreadsheet."
- "Honest. Not a pitch."

How we do **not** want to be seen:

- Impressive tech demo with no clear use.
- "Disruptive" or "revolutionary."
- A company that wants their data or their money more than their success.

---

## 8. Brand Principles

Timeless rules for every brand decision. These govern presentation; they do not replace the
product principles in `COMPANY_CONTEXT.md §8`.

1. **Restraint over decoration.** Remove until only the essential remains. Empty space is a
   feature.
2. **Clarity over cleverness.** Say the plain thing. A founder should never re-read a sentence
   to understand it.
3. **Calm confidence.** Quiet is stronger than loud. We do not raise our voice to be noticed.
4. **Respect attention.** The founder's attention is scarce. We earn it with usefulness, not
   tricks.
5. **Craft is non-negotiable.** Details - spacing, wording, capitalization - signal whether we
   care. We do.
6. **Empathy for the non-expert.** Meet the founder where they are. Never make them feel
   small for not knowing.
7. **Consistency compounds trust.** One voice, one terminology, everywhere. Drift reads as
   carelessness.
8. **Show, do not shout.** Demonstrate the calm answer; do not claim greatness.

## 9. Communication Principles

How we communicate, derived from `COMPANY_CONTEXT.md` (Writing Style) and `GLOSSARY.md`
(banned terminology):

- Use short sentences and plain English.
- Lead with the founder's problem, not our features.
- Use evidence and concrete outcomes instead of adjectives.
- Use the exact terminology in `GLOSSARY.md`; capitalize it as specified there.
- Never use the banned words in `GLOSSARY.md` (AI-powered, revolutionary, game-changing,
  cutting-edge, disruptive, best-in-class, and the misuse of dashboard or agent).
- Write to "you," the founder. Not to "users" or "the market."

---

## 10. Writing Philosophy

Write like a practical human who knows the founder's business.

- Say what the product does. "Business OS shows why your Instagram reach dropped" - not "unlock
  actionable intelligence."
- The reader should finish thinking "that is exactly my problem," not "what does that mean?"
- Concrete beats clever. A specific example is worth ten adjectives.
- If a sentence sounds like a press release, rewrite it.
- Never explain the technology to sound advanced. Explain the outcome to be useful.

---

## 11. Visual Philosophy (high level only)

Stated at principle level; no implementation detail.

- **Calm and minimal.** Generous whitespace, restrained color, no decorative noise.
- **One focal point per screen.** Never overwhelm; this is the visual form of "one clear answer
  beats ten charts" from `COMPANY_CONTEXT.md`.
- **Typography-led.** Hierarchy comes from type and space, not from effects.
- **Quiet in light and dark.** Both modes are calm, never high-contrast for drama.
- **Motion only when it aids understanding.** No motion for show.
- **Honest data.** Visualizations show one clear thing; they do not inflate or perform.

---

## 12. Brand Consistency Rules

- Use `GLOSSARY.md` terminology and capitalization in every surface: UI, website, social,
  email, docs.
- One voice across all channels. A founder should not hear a different company on Instagram
  than in the product.
- Frame everything from the Founder's ownership: their business, their data, their decision.
- Present Local First as a benefit, never a limitation.
- Prefer one clear idea per asset over a feature list.
- When in doubt, choose the calmer, plainer option.

---

## 13. Things We Never Do

- Use hype words from the `GLOSSARY.md` banned list.
- Create fake urgency (countdowns, "last chance") to push a solo founder.
- Call ourselves "AI-powered," "best-in-class," "revolutionary," or "disruptive."
- Lead with a dashboard screenshot when the product's heart is the Conversation.
- Use dark patterns, conflation, or guilt to keep a founder.
- Exaggerate capability. If we cannot demonstrate it this sprint, we do not claim it.
- Share or imply a founder's private data in examples or testimonials.
- Talk down to a non-technical founder. Ever.
- Compete on feature count. We compete on calm.

---

## 14. Brand Checklist

Every brand asset must pass before it ships:

- [ ] Uses `GLOSSARY.md` terminology and capitalization correctly.
- [ ] Contains none of the banned words.
- [ ] Reads calm, clear, practical, trustworthy, intelligent, minimal.
- [ ] Leads with the founder's problem, not our features.
- [ ] Shows Local First as a strength.
- [ ] Communicates one clear idea, not a feature dump.
- [ ] Would a non-technical solo founder understand it without help?
- [ ] Aligns with the brand personality in `COMPANY_CONTEXT.md` and the 8 principles.
- [ ] Demonstrates something real; claims nothing we cannot show this sprint.
- [ ] Sounds like the same company everywhere else we publish.
